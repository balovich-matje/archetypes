# Marksman node icons — specs

One 32x32 icon per family (MINOR skipped), built by `make.py` from vanilla
sprites pulled straight out of the client jar plus a handful of
hand-plotted effect pixels. Every icon starts from the family's own
assigned vanilla item (see `MarksmanNodes.Family`) so it sits next to
Minecraft's own art without looking foreign. Order below matches the
constellation: the bottom tip, up the string (crossbow branch), up the
stave (bow branch), the shared shaft, the top tip.

**true_shot** — the Spectral Arrow (already vanilla's own glowing arrow),
sized down and tucked bottom-left so its own flight line has room: a bold
gold dash continues dead straight off the tip clear across the top-right
corner to a bright flare. A normal shot would already be arcing down by
here; this one refuses to bend, and lands twice as hard.

**piercing_tips** — a whole, dimmed iron chestplate tucked top-left with a
small dark hole punched in its lower edge, a dashed line running clear of
the plate's own silhouette down to the family's assigned icon — a big iron
nugget — sitting past it at bottom-right: the tip that came out the other
side instead of stopping at the armour.

**rapid_reload** — the crossbow at rest with the family's assigned icon —
a cluster of vanilla's own redstone shard — mounted at the stock, red and
gold sparks jumping off the trigger: the reload charged by the kill that
just fed it.

**pinning** — the cobweb (the family's assigned icon, vanilla's own "this
slows you" prop) filling the frame, vanilla's own Slowness status icon
badged in the corner on a small dark chip so it doesn't dissolve into the
web's white lattice — the exact effect the hit applies, not just a spooky
prop.

**combustion** — the fire charge (the family's assigned icon), shrunk to
leave a clear ring of frame around it, a small grey arrowhead stuck into
its top edge and a burst of blast-ticks thrown well clear of the
fireball's own ember texture on all sides: the burning target it just
detonated, not a fireball sitting idle in the corner.

**snap_shot** — the crossbow already loaded (vanilla's own crossbow_arrow
pose — nothing left to draw, nothing left to load) with a wide, bright
muzzle burst at the front: the capstone shot that leaves the instant you
press the button.

**acrobatics** — the rabbit foot (the family's assigned icon — vanilla's
own jump/agility charm), pushed up-right with two short, crisp motion
dashes off its low edge and a small dust puff underneath: a quick
hop-roll, blunter and shorter than Swift Flight's sustained streaks.

**nimble_draw** — a bow at full, held draw (vanilla's own bow_pulling_2
pose, the taut triangle every player already reads as "string all the way
back"), sized down to free a clear strip at the bottom for a bold gold
motion trail: still moving, string still drawn, none of the usual crawl.

**swift_flight** — the feather (the family's assigned icon) with speed-line
dashes streaming off its trailing edge — the same Velocity motif the
Wizard tree already uses for the same "projectile flies faster" mechanic,
on the same anchor sprite, reused on purpose.

**seeker_arrow** — the Eye of Ender (the family's assigned icon —
vanilla's own "seeks the stronghold" homer) on a bent dashed path curving
hard toward a small red target mark: the capstone shot that steers itself
at the nearest hostile.

**conservation** — a fired arrow (the family's assigned icon) with a faint
ghost twin drifting back behind it, low and left of the shot — the house's
established "extra copy" grammar (Wizard's Echo, Slayer's Bulwark), here
standing for the shot that comes back to you instead of breaking.

**focus** — the spyglass (the family's assigned icon) with a small vanilla
clock badged in the corner and a red minus dash beside it — the same
reduction mark the Wizard tree's Clarity puts on its mana orb, here
shaving time off a cooldown instead of cost off a cast.
